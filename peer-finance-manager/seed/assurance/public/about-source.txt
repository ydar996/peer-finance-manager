
ASSURANCE INVESTMENT AND COOPERATIVE INC.(AIC)
Summary
 Assurance Investment and Cooperative Inc. is a member-owned cooperative society that aims to provide financial independence and mutual support to its members through regular financial contributions, lending to member, investments in REIT, and investment in real estate. The gain from the investments is distributed back to members based on their contributions. AIC’s immediate goal is to invest contributions in a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT), while its short-term goal is to start lending money to members. The one-year plan is to purchase a property for resale.

Mission Statement
 	Assurance Investment and Cooperative Inc. is a member-owned cooperative society dedicated to promoting financial independence and mutual support for its members through regular financial contributions and investment. We help members access fund with lower or minimal interest rate compared to banks and other financial institutions.

Overview
 Assurance Investment and Cooperative Inc. is a cooperative society that will benefit its members by providing access to passive income through investments in real estate. Membership is open to both men and women, although the cooperative will initially focus on recruiting men. Members are required to attend meetings and commit financially to the cooperative.

Membership
Membership in Assurance Investment and Cooperative Inc. is open to all, although the cooperative will concentrate on recruiting men initially. Members are required to attend meetings and commit financially to the cooperative. An upfront intake deposit amount of $100 is required, and members are expected to make a minimum monthly payment of $50. Payments can be made in installments of $50, $100, $200, $300, $400, $500, or any amount not more than $3000 per month.
Members of the cooperative are expected to:
Contribute the agreed-upon amount monthly, based on the payment plan they have chosen.
Members are required to commit financially to the cooperative.
Members are required to sign the cooperative’s bylaws 
Attend members' meetings, which may be held on a regular basis, to stay up-to-date on the cooperative's activities and participate in decision-making.
Volunteer for committees, which may be formed to address specific issues or initiatives within the cooperative.
Participate in voting, which may take place during members' meetings or via other channels, to make decisions that affect the cooperative and its members.
By fulfilling these responsibilities, members of the cooperative can help ensure its success and contribute to the collective benefits it provides to its members.


Goals
Immediate goal – AIC’s immediate goal is to invest contributions in a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) that will provide members with passive income. The cooperative will seek to invest in high-yielding REITs that will provide members with regular income.

Short-term goal – 
In six months, AIC plans to start lending money to members. This lending will be based on the amount contributed by the member, and the repayment will be scheduled based on the income generated by the member's contribution. The cooperative will also aim to establish relationships with local credit unions and banks to provide members with access to additional funding.
AIC aims to purchase a property for resale. This will generate income for the cooperative, which will be distributed among members based on their contributions. AIC will work with local real estate agents to identify potential properties for purchase, with a focus on properties that have the potential for high returns. Long-term plan 
Over the long term, AIC aims to expand its operations to include a wider range of investment options, including stocks,S &amp; P 500, bonds, and other financial instruments. AIC will also seek to establish partnerships with other cooperatives and financial institutions to provide members with additional resources and opportunities.

Benefits of joining AIC
Joining Assurance investment and cooperative inc. can provide numerous benefits for individuals. Here are some of the benefits.

Ownership and control: When you join an assurance cooperative, you become a member and part-owner of the organization. This means you have a say in how the cooperative is run and how profits are distributed.
Access to financial services: Assurance cooperatives offer a wide range of financial services, including savings and loans, and insurance products. For individuals who may not have access to these services through traditional banks, joining a cooperative can be a way to gain access to financial services and improve their financial well-being.
Diversification: Your money is automatically diversified across a range of assets, which can help reduce risk.
Shared knowledge and expertise: Investment clubs bring together people with different backgrounds and areas of expertise, which can result in a more diverse and well-rounded investment portfolio. Members can share their knowledge and insights, and everyone can learn from each other.
Reduced risk: By pooling resources, AIC can afford to invest in a more diverse portfolio, which can help reduce risk. Additionally, members can share the responsibility of researching and analyzing investments, which can help reduce the risk of making poor investment decisions.
Lower costs: Because AIC is not-for-profit organization, we able to offer lower fees and interest rates than traditional banks and financial institutions.
Social benefits: AIC can provide a social outlet for members, as we meet regularly to discuss issues affecting our cooperative and our members thereby build a sense of community.
Potential for higher returns: By investing in a diversified portfolio and pooling resources, AIC has the potential to achieve higher returns than individual.
Own a piece of Corporate America: We invest in corporate America and thus have a claim to share of the companies’ profit.
Build a lifetime of financial success: Financial success grow out of the assets we build over a period of time. Invest brings a deeper and more lasting reward of security and economic power that comes from accumulation of wealth. 
Education and support: AIC offer financial education and support services to our members, which can help individuals improve their financial literacy and make better financial decisions. Members can share resources, attend educational seminars, and learn from the experiences of other members.
Assume a mortgage: AIC can try to get fixed mortgage for members from local mortgage banks
